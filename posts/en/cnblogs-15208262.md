### Mean value theorems involving functions

Premise: `f(x)` is continuous on `[a,b]`.

#### Boundedness and extreme value theorem

`m ≤ f(x) ≤ M`, where `m` and `M` are respectively the minimum and maximum values of `f(x)` on `[a,b]`.

The interval must be closed.

#### Intermediate value theorem

When `m ≤ μ ≤ M`, there exists `ξ∈[a,b]` such that `f(ξ)=μ`.

#### Zero theorem

When `f(a)·f(b)<0`, there exists `ξ∈(a,b)` such that `f(ξ)=0`.

`f(a)` and `f(b)` have opposite signs.

`ξ` cannot be equal to `a` or `b`, because then `f(a)·f(b)=0`.
