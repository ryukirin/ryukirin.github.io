### Sequence limits

### Subsequences

A subsequence is formed by taking infinitely many terms from the original sequence while preserving their original order.

- If the original sequence converges, then every subsequence also converges.

- If a subsequence converges, it does not imply that the original sequence converges.

### Properties of convergent sequences

- Uniqueness

- Boundedness, because a convergent sequence has a fixed trend, not merely local boundedness

- Sign preservation

### Rules for limit operations

![](/assets/posts/cnblogs-15170660/image-03.png)

- The limit of a sum or difference equals the sum or difference of the limits.

- The limit of a product equals the product of the limits.

- The limit of a quotient equals the quotient of the limits.

### Proving that a limit exists

#### Squeeze theorem

If sequences `{xn}`, `{yn}`, and `{zn}` satisfy the following, where `n` is the subscript:

- `yn ≤ xn ≤ zn`

- `lim yn = a`, `lim zn = a`

then the limit of sequence `{xn}` exists and equals `a`.

#### Monotone bounded convergence criterion

A monotone bounded sequence must have a limit. That is, if sequence `{xn}` is monotonically increasing (or decreasing) and has an upper bound (or lower bound), then `lim xn` exists.
